Dental care



Diseases related to the oral cavity are widespread in all animals, and in some species and breeds are more common / tartar and retained deciduous teeth in dogs of small breeds /. The most common problems encountered by the team of the clinic are:

- Milk teeth - retained deciduous teeth create many problems when not removed in time - food is retained between them and permanent teeth, which is a prerequisite for the development of caries and gingivitis. The retention of deciduous teeth leads to the displacement of permanent teeth from their normal position.

- Tartar - tartar is porous and is an ideal environment for the development of a large number of microorganisms that release toxins and lead to inflammation of the gums. The accumulation of tartar between the gum and the base of the teeth leads to loosening of the tooth-crown connections, during which the teeth begin to shake. To avoid the accumulation of tartar, we recommend that you brush your pet's teeth several times a week. If you have already accumulated tartar in our clinic, it can be removed with a modern ultrasonic scaler.

- Tumors - tumors in the oral cavity are most often malignant and affect not only the soft tissues / gums / and the underlying bones of the jaws. For this reason, their early removal is extremely important.
